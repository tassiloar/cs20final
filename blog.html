Atomic Habits by James Clear

"Okay, Atomic Habits? Lowkey gave me hope I could fix my life. You know how you say, 'I'll start the gym tomorrow,' but tomorrow never shows up? Well, this book gave me the push to make 'tomorrow' happen. Clear's whole '1% better every day' thing makes change feel actually doable, like a Python loop instead of an endless while loop with no exit. Plus, the science of habit formation is kinda cool - almost makes me wanna switch to psych (jk). Solid read if you're juggling multiple deadlines and hoping to develop better sleep habits. 9/10 but I still procrastinated finishing it"

- Oliver Callahan

Beloved by Toni Morrison

"Listen, Beloved is not light reading. I started it thinking I'd skim, but Morrison pulls you in with haunting prose and themes that make your brain itch. The way it deals with trauma, memory, and identity? Wild. I won't lie, it took me way longer to finish than I planned, especially with a midterm breathing down my neck. But once you get into it, it's worth every brain cell. Just don't read it all in one sitting unless you wanna spiral into some deep reflection about past, present, and future. 9/10, but be prepared to feel things you weren't ready for."

- Claudine Hays

Dune by Frank Herbert

"First of all, Dune is like a fever dream. The world-building? Insane. I had to keep a notebook next to me just to remember who's who and what's what. But there's something about Herbert's weird, sprawling narrative that hooks you in. It's like the Game of Thrones of sci-fi: full of politics, betrayal, and spice (literally). Definitely not a 'quick read between classes' kinda book, though. But if you've got some time and like pondering life, power, and sandworms, it's worth the grind. 8/10, but you might need caffeine to get through it."

- Megan Bryant

The Alchemist by Paulo Coelho

"I picked this up because half the people in my dorm rave about it like it's gospel. It's a short read, but Coelho's whole 'personal legend' concept got me thinking about my major and whether I'm actually on the right path (spoiler: I have no idea). Santiago's journey of self-discovery felt a bit too smooth, though. Like, bro, real life isn't that deep — or is it? I'm still not sure. But it's one of those books that makes you feel better about not having your life together, so that's a win. 8/10, but don't expect it to solve all your problems."

- Eileen Holder

How to Win Friends and Influence People by Dale Carnegie

"Not gonna lie, I picked this up hoping it would teach me how to network better. And... it kinda did? Some of the advice is basic ('smile more' — okay, Dale), but other tips on empathy and listening actually work. If you’ve got anxiety about networking events or interviews, this book is like a cheat sheet for making small talk without sounding awkward. It’s a bit old-school, but hey, some strategies are timeless. 7/10 — definitely helpful, but also made me realize how much effort socializing takes."

- Gavin Moreno
